Can you explain the concept of backpropagation simply?

AI

StudyGPT 10:24 AM

Certainly. Think of backpropagation as a way for the network to learn from its mistakes.

The Analogy:

Imagine you are practising shooting a basketball. You take a shot and miss to the right. Your brain (the optimizer) realizes the error (loss) and tells your muscles to adjust slightly to the left for the next shot. You repeat this until you make the basket.

In the Neural Network:

  1. Forward Pass: The network makes a prediction (the shot).
  2. Loss Calculation: It calculates how far off the prediction was from the truth (the miss).
  3. Backward Pass: It calculates the gradient (the adjustment needed) for every weight in the network, moving backward from the output to the input.
  4. Update: The weights are updated to reduce the error.
